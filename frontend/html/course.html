Biology 101

Biology 101, an introductory course in the field of biology, is designed to provide students with a fundamental understanding of the science of life. In this course, students explore a wide range of biological concepts, including cell structure and function, genetics, evolution, and ecology.

The course begins by delving into the fascinating world of cells, elucidating their intricate structures and functions. Students learn about the genetic code, inheritance, and how genes contribute to the diversity of life. The principles of evolution are discussed, exploring how species adapt and change over time through natural selection and other mechanisms.

Additionally, Biology 101 investigates the relationships between organisms and their environments, touching on topics such as ecosystems, ecological interactions, and the impact of human activity on the environment. Throughout the course, students gain a deeper appreciation for the interconnectedness of life on Earth and the critical role of biology in addressing global challenges, from conservation to healthcare.
